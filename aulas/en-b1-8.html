Dialogue 1 — The travel bug
EmmaHave you ever travelled alone?
LucasYes, I have. I went to Chile last year on my own.
EmmaReally? Weren't you nervous?
LucasAt first, yes. When I first arrived, I experienced real culture shock.
EmmaI can imagine. How did you feel later?
LucasAfter a few weeks, I started loving it. I think I've become more independent because of that trip.
EmmaThat's amazing. I've always wanted to travel alone.
LucasYou should try it someday. Once you're bitten by the travel bug, you never want to stop exploring.
Dialogue 2 — Travel stories
AnaHave you visited many countries?
MeiNot really, but I've travelled around my country a lot.
AnaThat still counts.
MeiI've been to small towns, beaches, mountains… and I've learned a lot from those experiences.
AnaWhat was your favourite place?
MeiProbably a small village near the sea. I spent a month there a few years ago.
AnaThat sounds relaxing.
MeiIt was. I had never felt so calm before.
Dialogue 3 — Missing home
MarkHave you ever lived abroad?
JelenaYes, I have. I lived in Canada for six months.
MarkDid you enjoy it?
JelenaYes, but the first few weeks were difficult. I was really homesick.
MarkThat's understandable.
JelenaI missed my family like mad.
MarkDid things improve later?
JelenaDefinitely. I made friends and slowly got used to the new culture.
MarkI think travelling changes people a lot.
JelenaI completely agree.

Grammar

1. Present Perfect Simple

Used for past actions with a connection to the present. Focus on experience and result.

I've travelled a lot. → Eu viajei muito.

She's visited many countries. → Ela visitou muitos países.

We've learned many things. → Nós aprendemos muitas coisas.

2. Present Perfect Continuous

Used for actions that started in the past and are still ongoing. Focus on duration and continuous activity.

I've been living abroad for six months. → Estou morando no exterior há seis meses.

She's been studying English since January. → Ela estuda inglês desde janeiro.

3. Since & For

Since = ponto inicial no tempo.

since 2023 / since last month

For = duração de tempo.

for two weeks / for a long time

4. Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Present Perfect = experiência geral, sem tempo definido.

I've visited Spain. → Eu já visitei a Espanha.

Past Simple = momento definido no passado.

I visited Spain last year. → Eu visitei a Espanha no ano passado.
