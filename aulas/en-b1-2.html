Dialogue 1 — Talking to new people
LucasDo you find it easy to talk to new people?
EmmaIt depends. Sometimes I feel confident, but other times I hesitate.
LucasSame here. I try not to stumble over my words.
EmmaYeah, that’s the worst. I also hate mumbling when I’m nervous.
LucasMe too. I think it’s important to speak clearly and smile.
EmmaDefinitely. That helps you make a good impression.
LucasI agree. People usually react better when you sound interested.
Dialogue 2 — Communication problems
MeiYesterday, I met someone new, but the conversation was awkward.
AnaWhat happened?
MeiI didn’t know what to say, so I just kept quiet.
AnaThat happens. Did you try to ask questions?
MeiNot really… I think I talked down to her without meaning to.
AnaOh, be careful with that. People don’t like that.
MeiI know… I didn’t mean it like that.
AnaNext time, just show interest and listen more.
MeiI’ll try. I want to improve my communication.
Dialogue 3 — Giving advice
MarkI sometimes feel nervous when I speak to new colleagues.
JelenaThat’s normal. Just be yourself.
MarkI also speak too fast sometimes.
JelenaThen slow down. And don’t mumble.
MarkGood point.
JelenaAlso, don’t talk about yourself too much.
MarkRight… I don’t want to sound arrogant or boast.
JelenaExactly. Ask questions and let the other person speak.
MarkThat makes sense. I think I’ll feel more comfortable next time.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Use Present Simple for habits and general situations. Use Present Continuous for things happening now or temporarily.

I speak too fast. (habit)

I’m speaking to someone new. (now)

I usually ask questions. (habit)

I’m trying to improve my communication. (temporary situation)

2. Advice structures

Use these structures to give advice in a simple and natural way.

You should speak clearly.

You shouldn’t mumble.

Try to ask questions.

Don’t talk too much.

3. Imperative

Use the imperative to give direct advice, instructions, or warnings.

Speak up.

Don’t gossip.

Be yourself.

Ask questions.

4. Language of interaction

These expressions help you react naturally and keep a conversation going.

I think it’s important to speak clearly.

I agree.

That makes sense.

Good point.
