Dialogues
Dialogue 1 — Family arguments
MandyWhy is your brother upset again?
GillHe had a huge argument with our dad yesterday.
MandyReally? What happened?
GillMy dad told him to stop going out so much, and he got angry.
MandyThat doesn’t surprise me. He’s always been stubborn.
GillTrue. They usually get on well, but sometimes they argue about everything.
MandyFamilies are complicated sometimes.
GillDefinitely. But they’ll probably make up soon.
Dialogue 2 — Talking about relationships
EmmaAre you still going out with Kevin?
LucasNo, we split up last month.
EmmaOh no… I’m sorry to hear that.
LucasIt’s okay. We had been having problems for a while.
EmmaThat must have been difficult.
LucasYeah, but I think it was the right decision.
EmmaDo you still keep in touch with him?
LucasNot really. We don’t talk much anymore.
Dialogue 3 — Giving advice
MarkI forgot to reply to my boss’s message yesterday.
JelenaYou should have answered earlier.
MarkI know… I was too tired when I got home.
JelenaYou shouldn’t ignore work messages completely.
MarkTrue. I also forgot to turn off my phone during dinner.
JelenaThat’s becoming a habit.
MarkYeah… I think I need to disconnect more often.
JelenaProbably. You don’t have to be available all the time.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. Expressing ability

could / was able to

I could read when I was four.

She was able to solve the problem.

Negative:

couldn’t

wasn’t able to

2. Obligation & necessity

have to / had to

I have to reply to messages.

We had to leave early.

don’t have to

You don’t have to answer immediately.

3. Advice & criticism

should / shouldn’t

You should disconnect more often.

People shouldn’t use phones all the time.

should have / shouldn’t have = criticism or regret

You should have replied earlier.

I shouldn’t have ignored the message.

4. Connected speech

Common spoken English connections:

Can you → /kənju/

Did you → /dɪdʒu/

Have to → /hæftə/

Important for listening comprehension.
