Dialogue 1 — A difficult experience
EmmaHave you ever done something really challenging?
LucasYes. A few years ago, I worked in a wildlife sanctuary for two months.
EmmaThat sounds fascinating.
LucasIt was, but it was also exhausting sometimes.
EmmaWhat was the hardest part?
LucasAt first, I found it difficult to get used to the routine.
EmmaI can imagine.
LucasSome tasks were pretty disgusting too, especially cleaning animal areas.
EmmaThat must have been unpleasant.
LucasIt was, but the experience was incredibly rewarding.
Dialogue 2 — Talking about Bhutan
AnaHave you ever heard of Bhutan?
MeiYes, I have. It seems like a very peaceful country.
AnaExactly. The people there believe happiness is more important than money.
MeiReally? That’s inspiring.
AnaThey also protect nature and limit tourism carefully.
MeiThat’s unusual nowadays.
AnaI agree. They want to avoid mass tourism and protect their culture.
MeiHonestly, I’d love to visit someday.
Dialogue 3 — Learning from experiences
MarkHave you ever had a life-changing experience?
JelenaDefinitely. Living abroad changed me a lot.
MarkIn what way?
JelenaI became more independent and open-minded.
MarkWas it difficult at first?
JelenaVery. I felt homesick and overwhelmed sometimes.
MarkBut you adapted eventually?
JelenaYes. Over time, I got used to everything.
MarkI think difficult experiences often teach us the most.
JelenaI completely agree with you.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. Adjectives ending in -ing vs -ed

-ing describes the situation.

The experience was fascinating.

The job was exhausting.

-ed describes feelings.

I was fascinated.

I felt exhausted.

2. Present Perfect review

Used for experience connected to the present.

I’ve worked abroad before.

She’s visited many countries.

Have you ever had a life-changing experience?

3. Used to

Used for a past habit or past situation.

I used to live in a small town.

I used to feel homesick.

4. Get used to

Used for adaptation.

I got used to the weather.

She’s getting used to the culture.

I found it difficult to get used to the routine.

5. Expressing opinion naturally

I think…

I honestly believe…

In my opinion…

I completely agree…
