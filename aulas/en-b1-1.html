Dialogue 1 — First impressions
EmmaDo you think first impressions are important?
LucasYes, I think they are. Most people make a judgement about someone when they first meet them.
EmmaI agree, but I don't think they are always correct.
LucasThat's true. I'm reading a book about it at the moment.
EmmaReally? What does it say?
LucasIt says we make decisions without even thinking.
EmmaThat makes sense. I usually try to come across as confident.
LucasMe too, but sometimes I feel shy.
EmmaSame here. First impressions can be stressful.
LucasYeah… but I think they can change over time.
Dialogue 2 — Meeting new people
AnaDo you find it easy to talk to new people?
MeiNot really. I often feel nervous.
AnaWhen I meet someone new, I always smile and ask questions.
MeiThat's a good idea. I'm going to try that next time.
AnaIt helps. People usually like friendly conversations.
MeiI hope so. I don't want to make a bad impression.
AnaDon't worry. Just be yourself.
MeiI'll try. I think it'll be easier with practice.
AnaDefinitely. And try not to judge a book by its cover.
MeiYeah… I sometimes do that without realising.
Dialogue 3 — Social behaviour
MarkI meet new colleagues all the time at work.
JelenaDo you usually get along with them?
MarkMost of the time, yes. But sometimes I say the wrong thing.
JelenaThat happens. You should think before you speak.
MarkI know… I also find it difficult not to judge people quickly.
JelenaTry to listen more. It really helps.
MarkThat's true. I don't always see eye to eye with everyone.
JelenaThat's normal. You don't have to agree with everyone.
MarkRight. I just try to stay polite.
JelenaThat's the best thing you can do.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Use Present Simple for habits and general truths. Use Present Continuous for things happening now or temporarily.

I meet new people every week. (habit)

I'm meeting new people this week. (temporary)

I read books about psychology. (general)

I'm reading a book at the moment. (now)

2. Will vs Going to

Use I'll for decisions made in the moment. Use I'm going to for plans and intentions.

I'll try. (decision right now)

I'm going to try that next time. (plan)

I think it'll be easier with practice. (opinion / prediction)

I'm going to talk to her tomorrow. (intention)

3. Language of opinion (essential B1)

Express your view clearly — agree, disagree, or add nuance.

I think first impressions are important.

I don't think they are always correct.

I believe people judge too quickly.

I agree, but I think it can change over time.

4. Social expressions in context

These expressions are used to describe behaviour and relationships naturally.

come across as confident → how others perceive you

see eye to eye → to agree with someone

judge a book by its cover → to judge by appearance only
