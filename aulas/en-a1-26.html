How to... describe people

A: What does your brother look like? — B: He’s tall and thin.

A: Is she young? — B: Yes, she is.

A: Does he have blue eyes? — B: No, he doesn’t. He has brown eyes.

A: Is your sister tall? — B: No, she’s quite short.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. Describing people with TO BE

We use to be + adjective to describe people.

He’s tall.

She’s young.

They’re attractive.

My uncle is thin.

My aunt is short.

2. Questions with TO BE

Is he tall?

Is she young?

Are they attractive?

Short answers

Yes, he is.

No, she isn’t.

Yes, they are.

3. HAVE / HAS — Appearance

We use have / has to describe physical features.

I have blue eyes.

She has long hair.

He has brown eyes.

They have black hair.

My sister has blonde hair.

4. Review — Adjectives

AdjectiveOpposite
tallshort
youngold
attractiveugly
thinfat*

*In everyday English, fat can sound impolite. Native speakers often prefer overweight, heavy, or big depending on the situation.

5. Putting it together

He’s tall and thin.

She has long, blonde hair and blue eyes.

My uncle is old, but he looks young.

My niece is a baby. She has beautiful brown eyes.
