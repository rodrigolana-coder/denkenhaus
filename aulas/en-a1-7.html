How to talk about groups of people

A: Who are they? — B: They’re my friends.

A: Are they students? — B: Yes, they are.

A: Are you classmates? — B: Yes, we are.

A: Where are they from? — B: They’re from Canada.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. We / You / They + To Be

Affirmative

We are friends.

You are students.

They are classmates.

Negative

We aren’t teachers.

You aren’t from France.

They aren’t doctors.

Questions

Are we late?

Are you classmates?

Are they cousins?

2. You = Singular and Plural

The verb is the same.

You are my friend.

You are my friends.

Are you a teacher?

Are you teachers?

3. Contractions

We are → We’re

You are → You’re

They are → They’re

We’re classmates.

They’re cousins.

4. Possessive adjectives — Review

my · your · his · her · its · our · their

Our teacher is very nice.

Their parents are doctors.

Our classmates are from Brazil.

Their cousins are Canadian.

5. Possessive ’s — Review

Emma’s cousin

Lucas’s brother

Daniel’s parents

Sophie’s teacher
