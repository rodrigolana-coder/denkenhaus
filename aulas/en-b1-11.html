Dialogue 1 — Different ways of learning
EmmaDo you think everyone learns in the same way?
LucasNot at all. Some people learn faster when they study alone, while others prefer working in groups.
EmmaThat’s true. I’m someone who needs a quiet environment to concentrate.
LucasReally? I actually get distracted when it’s too quiet.
EmmaInteresting. What helps you focus?
LucasListening to music sometimes helps me study better.
EmmaI could never do that. I need complete silence.
LucasI guess everyone develops different learning habits over time.
Dialogue 2 — School memories
AnaWhat subjects did you enjoy most at school?
MeiI loved literature and history.
AnaReally? I always preferred science subjects.
MeiHistory teachers were usually the ones who inspired me the most.
AnaI had a maths teacher who completely changed the way I saw learning.
MeiThat makes a huge difference. Good teachers can really motivate students.
AnaDefinitely. Some teachers only focus on exams instead of real understanding.
MeiI completely agree with that.
Dialogue 3 — Studying nowadays
MarkDo you think online learning is effective?
JelenaIt depends. Some students are motivated enough to study alone, but others struggle without structure.
MarkThat’s true. I sometimes procrastinate when I study from home.
JelenaSame here. It’s difficult to stay focused all the time.
MarkEspecially with phones and social media around.
JelenaExactly. People who manage their time well usually learn faster.
MarkI wish I were more organised sometimes.
JelenaHonestly, that’s a skill which takes time to develop.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. Relative clauses — who / which / that

Used to give extra information.

who → people

Teachers who motivate students are important.

People who study regularly improve faster.

which / that → things

This is the method that helps me most.

Online learning is something which works for many people.

2. Gerunds (-ing)

Used after certain verbs.

I enjoy studying alone.

She loves learning languages.

They avoid using their phones while studying.

3. Infinitives — to + verb

Used for purpose, intention and after many verbs.

I want to improve my English.

He decided to study abroad.

We need to concentrate more.

4. Gerund vs infinitive

Meaning can change depending on the form.

I stopped studying. → parei de estudar.

I stopped to study. → parei para estudar.
