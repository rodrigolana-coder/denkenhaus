Dialogues
Dialogue 1 — Staying in touch
EmmaAre you always on your phone too?
LucasHonestly, yes. I use it all the time to stay in touch with people.
EmmaSame here. I’d probably lose touch with some friends without it.
LucasExactly. I especially use it to keep in touch with my family.
EmmaDo you think people depend too much on phones nowadays?
LucasMaybe. Some people can’t even go out without checking their phones every five minutes.
EmmaThat’s true. I try not to use mine all the time.
Dialogue 2 — Phone habits
AnaWhen did you get your first mobile phone?
MeiI was about twelve, I think.
AnaThat’s quite young.
MeiYes, but my parents wanted to contact me after school.
AnaWhat about you?
MeiI got mine when I was fourteen.
AnaNowadays, children get phones earlier and earlier.
Dialogue 3 — Technology and life
MarkI think people spend too much time online.
JelenaI agree to some extent.
MarkSome people can’t even have dinner without checking their phones.
JelenaThat’s true. We should disconnect more often.
MarkPhones are useful, but people shouldn’t become obsessed with them.
JelenaExactly. Technology should help us, not control us.

Grammar

1. Verb + infinitive

I want to contact my friends.

People need to disconnect sometimes.

2. Modal verbs

People can communicate easily.

You shouldn’t use your phone constantly.

We should spend more time face to face.

3. Frequency expressions

all the time

every five minutes

more often

4. Present simple for habits

I use my phone every day.

She checks her messages constantly.
