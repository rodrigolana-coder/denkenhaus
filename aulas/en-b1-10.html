Dialogue 1 — Dream jobs
EmmaWhat would your ideal job look like?
LucasHonestly, I’d like to have a job that allows me to travel.
EmmaThat sounds exciting.
LucasYeah, I don’t think I’d enjoy working in the same office every day.
EmmaReally? I’d actually prefer something more stable.
LucasFair enough. Would you rather work alone or in a team?
EmmaProbably in a team, as long as the environment was friendly.
LucasSame here. I think work relationships are really important.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. Would for preferences & desires

would like

I’d like to work abroad.

She’d like to study design.

would rather

I’d rather work from home.

He’d rather travel alone.

2. Hypothetical situations

Used for imaginary or unreal situations.

I’d travel more if I had more free time.

She’d move abroad if she had the chance.

3. Second Conditional

Structure:

If + Past Simple → would + verb

If I had more money, I’d travel more.

If I lived abroad, I’d learn faster.

Used for imaginary situations, dreams and speculation.

4. Giving opinions naturally

I think I’d…

I’d probably…

Honestly, I’d…

These expressions make speech sound more natural and fluent.
