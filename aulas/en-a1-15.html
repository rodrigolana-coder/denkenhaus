Tea Time: A British Tradition
In the United Kingdom, tea is much more than a drink.
It is an important part of everyday life and one of the country’s most famous traditions.
Many British people drink tea every day.
Some people have two or three cups, and others drink even more.
Tea is popular at home, at work, and in cafés.
One famous tradition is afternoon tea.
It usually includes tea, small sandwiches, cakes, and biscuits.
People often enjoy afternoon tea with friends or family.
Although coffee is becoming more popular, tea is still the favourite hot drink for many people in the UK.
When you visit Britain, someone may ask: “Would you like a cup of tea?”
For many British people, sharing a cup of tea is a friendly way to welcome guests and spend time together.
