The Many Englishes Around the World
Did you know that English is spoken in many different countries?
People often think that English is only spoken in the United Kingdom or the United States, but it is also an official language in countries like Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Ireland, South Africa and many others.
Although people speak the same language, their accents, vocabulary and even some expressions can be different.
For example, in the UK people usually say “flat”, while in the US they say “apartment”.
In the UK, people often ask for the “lift”, but Americans usually say “elevator”.
The same happens with food. In Britain, people eat chips, while Americans often call them French fries. In the UK, crisps are what Americans call potato chips.
Another interesting fact is that English has words from many other languages, including French, Latin, German, Spanish and even Hindi.
Today, more than one and a half billion people speak English or are learning it around the world.
For many people, English is the language of travel, business, music, films and the internet.
Wherever you go, speaking English can help you meet new people and discover new cultures.
