Why Do People Drive on the Left in the UK?
If you visit the United Kingdom, you will notice something different: people drive on the left side of the road.
Only about 35% of the world’s countries drive on the left, but the UK is one of them.
Many historians believe this tradition began hundreds of years ago.
Most people were right-handed, so riding or walking on the left made it easier to protect themselves and greet other people safely.
Today, British drivers still drive on the left, and the steering wheel is usually on the right side of the car.
For tourists, this can be confusing at first.
When crossing the street, people often need to remember to look right first, not left.
Many visitors write “LOOK RIGHT” on their hands or phones to help them remember.
Driving on the left may seem strange, but after a few days, most people get used to it.
