Section 2 of 5
Grammar — Position of Adverbs

1. With normal verbs — Present Simple

Adverbs of frequency usually come before the main verb.

SubjectAdverbVerb
Ialwaysstudy
Sheusuallyworks
Theyrarelytravel

I always drink coffee in the morning.

She usually studies after dinner.

They rarely watch television.

2. With the verb “to be”

The adverb comes after the verb “to be”.

SubjectTo beAdverb
Heisalways
Weareusually
Iamnever

He is always tired.

We are usually busy.

I am never late.

3. With Present Perfect

The adverb normally comes between have / has and the past participle.

Subjecthave/hasAdverbPast participle
Ihavealwaysloved
Shehasnevervisited
Theyhavealreadyfinished

I have always liked languages.

She has never visited Canada.

We have already finished the lesson.

He has just arrived.

They have recently moved here.

4. With Past Perfect

The adverb normally comes between had and the past participle.

SubjecthadAdverbPast participle
Ihadalreadyeaten
Shehadneverseen
Theyhadalwaysstudied

I had already left when she called.

She had never travelled abroad before.

They had always wanted to study English.

We had just finished dinner.
