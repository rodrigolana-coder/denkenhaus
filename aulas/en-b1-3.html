Dialogues
Dialogue 1 — Talking about family
EmmaDo you have a big family?
LucasYes, I do. I grew up with three siblings.
EmmaReally? That must have been fun.
LucasIt was, but sometimes it was chaotic.
EmmaI can imagine. Do you keep in touch with all of them?
LucasYes, I do. We’re quite close.
EmmaThat’s nice. I think family relationships are very important.
LucasI agree, even if you don’t always see eye to eye.
Dialogue 2 — A family story
MeiI listened to a story about a family who worked together.
AnaWhat did they do?
MeiThey were jugglers. The father taught his children everything.
AnaThat sounds interesting.
MeiYes, they had started performing when the children were very young.
AnaThat must have required a lot of practice.
MeiDefinitely. They were training every day while other kids were playing.
AnaWow… that’s a very different childhood.
MeiYes, but they seemed very happy.
Dialogue 3 — Talking about the past
MarkWhat were you like as a child?
JelenaI was quite shy, actually.
MarkReally? I can’t imagine that.
JelenaYes, I didn’t use to talk much.
MarkAnd now you’re very confident.
JelenaI changed over time.
MarkI think experiences shape people a lot.
JelenaI agree. What about you?
MarkI was always curious and talkative.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. Past Simple

Use Past Simple for completed actions in the past.

I grew up in a big family.

They worked together.

He taught his children.

2. Past Continuous

Use Past Continuous for an action in progress in the past.

They were training every day.

He was working while they were sleeping.

Use it with: while / when.

3. Past Perfect

Use Past Perfect for an action that happened before another action in the past.

They had started very young.

He had learned the basics before teaching.

4. Key structure

This is a very important B1 combination.

He was working while his children were sleeping.

After he had learned, he started teaching.

5. Used to

Use used to for past habits or past states.

I used to be shy.

I didn’t use to talk much.
