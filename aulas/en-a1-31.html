How to... ask about someone’s life

A: What do you do at the weekend? — B: We usually visit our grandparents.

A: When do you study English? — B: On Mondays and Wednesdays.

A: Who do you work with? — B: I work with my friends.

A: Where do they live? — B: They live in Manchester.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. Present Simple — WE / YOU (plural) / THEY

We use these subjects to talk about groups of people.

We live in Brazil.

You study together.

They work in a hospital.

We play football every Saturday.

They don’t work on Sundays.

2. WH- questions — What / Where

What asks about things or activities.

What do you do?

What do they study?

What do you usually eat?

Where asks about places.

Where do you live?

Where do they work?

Where do you study?

3. WH- questions — When / Who / How

When asks about time.

When do you have English class?

When do you get up?

When do they finish work?

Who asks about people.

Who do you live with?

Who do you work with?

Who do you study with?

How asks about the way something happens.

How do you go to work?

How do they travel?

How do you study English?

4. Word order

WH-word + do + subject + verb

Where do you live?

What do they do?

When do we study?

Who do you work with?

How do they travel?

5. Review — Present Simple

We play football.

You don’t work here.

They like coffee.

We don’t study on Sundays.
