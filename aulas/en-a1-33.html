Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. HAVE GOT — I / You / We / They

We often use have got to talk about possessions and what we own.

Affirmative

I’ve got a sofa.

You’ve got a big kitchen.

We’ve got two bathrooms.

They’ve got a beautiful house.

Negative

I haven’t got a sofa.

You haven’t got a garden.

We haven’t got a garage.

They haven’t got a dishwasher.

Questions

Have you got a sofa?

Have they got a garden?

Have we got enough chairs?

Short answers

Yes, I have. / No, I haven’t.

Yes, they have. / No, they haven’t.

2. HAS GOT — He / She / It

Affirmative

He’s got a new desk.

She’s got a big wardrobe.

It’s got a small kitchen.

Negative

He hasn’t got a bath.

She hasn’t got a cooker.

It hasn’t got a garage.

Questions

Has he got a car?

Has she got a dishwasher?

Has it got a balcony?

Short answers

Yes, he has. / No, he hasn’t.

Yes, she has. / No, she hasn’t.

3. HAVE GOT × HAVE

Both forms are correct. British English often prefers have got.

I’ve got a sofa. / I have a sofa.

She’s got a washing machine. / She has a washing machine.

4. Review — There is / There are

There is a sofa in the living room.

There are four chairs around the table.

There isn’t a dishwasher.

Are there any mirrors?
