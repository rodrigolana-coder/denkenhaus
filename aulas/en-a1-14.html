How to order food

Waiter: Can I help you? — Customer: Yes, please.

Waiter: What would you like? — Customer: I’d like a chicken sandwich.

Waiter: Anything to drink? — Customer: An orange juice, please.

Waiter: Anything else? — Customer: No, thank you.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. I’d like…

We use I’d like… to order food politely.

I’d like a coffee.

I’d like a sandwich.

I’d like an orange juice.

We’d like two teas.

2. Can I have…?

Another polite way to order food.

Can I have a coffee?

Can I have a cheese sandwich?

Can I have two slices of cake?

3. Possessive ’s — Review

We use ’s to show possession.

Emma’s coffee

Daniel’s sandwich

Lucas’s orange juice

Sophie’s tea

4. Review — A / AN

a sandwich

a coffee

a cake

an orange juice
