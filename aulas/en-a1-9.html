How to talk about your things

A: What's in your backpack? — B: There's a notebook and a pen.

A: Is this your phone? — B: Yes, it is.

A: Whose camera is this? — B: It's Daniel's camera.

A: What do you have in your bag? — B: I have a laptop and two books.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. A / AN

We use a before consonant sounds.

a book

a pen

a backpack

a computer

a camera

We use an before vowel sounds.

an apple

an orange

an umbrella

2. Examples with A / AN

I have a book.

She has a camera.

He has an apple.

We have an umbrella.

3. Plural nouns

Most nouns: add -s.

book → books

pen → pens

apple → apples

computer → computers

passport → passports

4. Nouns ending in -s, -sh, -ch, -x

Add -es.

watch → watches

bus → buses

box → boxes

brush → brushes

5. Review — Possessive ’s

Daniel's phone

Emma's backpack

Lucas's laptop

Sophie's camera
