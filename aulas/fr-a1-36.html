Diálogos
Diálogo 1 — TV experiences
NoraHave you watched that new programme on TV?
EmmaYes, I have watched it. It has been very interesting.
NoraReally? I haven’t watched it yet. What is it about?
EmmaIt’s about the history of television and how it has changed over time.
NoraThat sounds good. Have you learned anything new?
EmmaYes, I have learned a lot about old TV sets and early programmes.
NoraHave they shown old televisions?
EmmaYes, they have shown some very old ones. The screens have been very small.
Diálogo 2 — Experiences
LucasHave you ever watched TV all day?
HugoYes, I have done that once when I was at home.
LucasReally? I have never done that. I get bored very quickly.
HugoI understand. I have watched many programmes, but not all of them have been good.
LucasHave you watched anything interesting recently?
HugoYes, I have watched an incredible film on a digital channel.
LucasHave you recommended it to anyone?
HugoYes, I have recommended it to my friends.
Diálogo 3 — Changes
EmmaTelevision has changed a lot in recent years.
HugoYes, it has changed a lot. I have noticed many differences.
EmmaHave you seen old television sets?
HugoYes, I have seen them in museums.
EmmaWhat have you noticed about them?
HugoThe screens have been very small and the image has not been very clear.
EmmaAnd now?
HugoNow, screens have become very large and the quality has improved a lot.

Gramática

1. Present Perfect

have / has + past participle

I have watched

She has seen

2. Use

👉 experiência

3. Negative

I haven’t watched it

4. Questions

Have you watched it?

5. Key words

ever / never / yet
