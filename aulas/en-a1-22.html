How to ask where a place is

A: Excuse me. Is there a bank near here? — B: Yes, there is. It’s next to the supermarket.

A: Are there any cafés nearby? — B: Yes, there are.

A: Where is the museum? — B: It’s opposite the park.

A: Where are the restaurants? — B: They’re near the train station.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. There isn’t / There aren’t

Singular

There isn’t a bank near here.

There isn’t a museum in my town.

There isn’t a café on this street.

Plural

There aren’t any parks nearby.

There aren’t any restaurants here.

There aren’t any hotels in this area.

2. Is there…? / Are there…?

Singular

Is there a supermarket?

Is there a bank near your house?

Is there a theatre in your city?

Plural

Are there any cafés nearby?

Are there any museums?

Are there any parks in your neighbourhood?

3. Any

We usually use any in questions and negative sentences.

Are there any restaurants?

Are there any hotels?

There aren’t any museums.

There isn’t any parking here.

4. Prepositions of place

The bank is next to the supermarket.

The café is near the station.

The museum is opposite the park.

The hotel is in front of the theatre.

The restaurant is on King Street.

The museum is in the city centre.
