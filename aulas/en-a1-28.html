Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. Present Simple — HE / SHE / IT

For he, she and it, most verbs add -s.

He works.

She lives.

It starts.

He likes music.

She reads every evening.

2. Verbs ending in -sh, -ch, -x, -o, -ss

These verbs usually add -es.

He watches TV.

She finishes work.

He goes to school.

She washes the car.

It fixes the problem.

3. HAVE → HAS

Have changes to has with he, she and it.

He has breakfast at seven.

She has two brothers.

My father has a new car.

4. Questions with DOES

Does he work here?

Does she like coffee?

Does it open at nine?

Short answers

Yes, he does.

No, he doesn’t.

Yes, she does.

No, she doesn’t.

5. Negative sentences

He doesn’t work on Sundays.

She doesn’t like tea.

It doesn’t open on Mondays.

6. Review — Adverbs of frequency

always

usually

often

sometimes

never

He usually gets up early.

She always has breakfast.

He often watches TV.

She never goes to bed late.
