Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. Adverbs of frequency

We use adverbs of frequency to say how often we do something.

FrequencyAdverb
100%always
90%usually
70%often
50%sometimes
10%rarely
0%never

I always make my bed.

She usually cooks dinner.

We often vacuum the house.

He sometimes washes the dishes.

They rarely iron their clothes.

I never clean the windows.

2. Position of adverbs

Adverbs of frequency usually come before the main verb.

I usually cook.

She always studies.

They often visit us.

We sometimes eat out.

With the verb to be, the adverb comes after the verb.

He is always happy.

They are usually busy.

She is never late.

3. HOW OFTEN...?

Use How often...? to ask about frequency.

How often do you cook?

How often does your father clean the house?

How often do you wash your car?

Common answers

Every day. Every morning. Every weekend.

Once a week. Twice a week. Three times a week.

Sometimes. Never.
