How to... describe your house

A: Do you live in a house or a flat? — B: I live in a flat.

A: How many bedrooms does it have? — B: It has three bedrooms.

A: Is there a garden? — B: Yes, there is.

A: What’s your favourite room? — B: My favourite room is the kitchen.

Section 2 of 5
Grammar

1. HAVE / HAS — Describing a house

Use have and has to describe what a house or flat contains.

I have a small flat.

We have three bedrooms.

It has two bathrooms.

My house has a big garden.

2. THERE IS / THERE ARE (review)

Use there is and there are to talk about what exists.

There is a kitchen.

There is a garage.

There are three bedrooms.

There are two bathrooms.

3. Questions with DOES

Does it have a garden?

Does your house have a garage?

Does your flat have a balcony?

Answers

Yes, it does.

No, it doesn’t.

4. Review — Prepositions of place
