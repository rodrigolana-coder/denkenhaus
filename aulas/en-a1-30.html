Why Do British People Say "Sorry" So Much?
If you visit the United Kingdom, you will probably hear the word "sorry" many times every day.
British people often say "sorry", even when they haven't done anything wrong.
For example, if two people walk into each other in a supermarket, both people usually say "sorry."
People also say "sorry" when they want to ask a question politely.
For example: "Sorry, where's the train station?"
Or when they want someone's attention: "Sorry, can I ask you something?"
In Britain, saying "sorry" is often a way to be polite and friendly.
Many visitors think British people apologise all the time, but for them, "sorry" is simply part of everyday conversation.
So, if you travel to the UK and someone says "sorry" to you, don't worry—they're probably just being polite!
